    Stochastic model
    
            
    The 2015 detection of GW150914 inaugurated gravitational-wave astronomy. Detected strain signals from compact-binary mergers — binary black holes (BBH), binary neutron stars (BNS), and neutron-star–black-hole pairs (NSBH) — encode the source's intrinsic and extrinsic parameters in their inspiral-merger-ringdown waveform. The inspiral phase admits an analytical post-Newtonian expansion that depends primarily on a combination of component masses called the chirp mass:
    
      
    M = (m_1 m_2)^{3/5} / (m_1 + m_2)^{1/5}
    
      
    The chirp mass uniquely determines the leading-order frequency evolution of the inspiral. To leading post-Newtonian order:
    
      
    f^{−8/3}(t) = (8/3) (5/256)^{8/3} (π G M / c³)^{5/3} (t_c − t)
    
      
    Here t_c is the coalescence time. This is the classical "chirp": as the binary inspirals, gravitational-wave frequency increases as the inverse 3/8 power of the time-to-coalescence. The strain amplitude scales as h ∝ M^{5/3} f^{2/3} / D_L where D_L is the luminosity distance.
    
      
    Higher post-Newtonian corrections introduce dependence on the mass ratio q = m_2/m_1 (≤1), the effective aligned spin χ_eff = (m_1 χ_{1z} + m_2 χ_{2z}) / (m_1 + m_2), and (for BNS) the tidal deformability parameter Λ̃. Eccentricity (≤0.3 at design horizon) and precession (spin misalignment) are higher-order effects.
    
      
    Parameter inference is fully Bayesian. Given a detected signal s(t) = h(t) + n(t) with detector noise n, the likelihood is:
    
      
    log L(θ) = −½ ⟨s − h(θ) | s − h(θ)⟩
    
      
    where ⟨·|·⟩ is the noise-weighted inner product. Nested sampling (LALInference) or stochastic-sampling (Bilby) generates posterior samples over θ = {M, q, χ_eff, D_L, RA, Dec, ψ, ι, t_c, φ_c}. The published GWTC-3 catalog reports the marginal posteriors over the intrinsic parameters of interest.
    

            
    Calibration
    
            
    Per-event median posteriors are seeded directly from the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA GWTC-3 catalog (Abbott et al. 2023) for events through the end of O3b. For O4a/O4b candidates (GW230529, GW231123, GW240817, GW250121) we use placeholder values pending the next-catalog finalization; the dashboard flags these clearly.
    
      
    The Q-transform spectrogram template (Chatterji et al. 2004) uses a synthetic Gaussian envelope in (log f, t) centered on the analytic chirp track f(t), with width σ proportional to the posterior parameter uncertainty. The corner plot diagonals are Gaussian marginals centered on the median, with σ from the GWTC-3 posterior columns; off-diagonals show 90% credible-region ellipses.
    
      
    Detection thresholds (FAR < 1/yr, network SNR ≥ 8, joint-detection across ≥2 detectors) follow the LIGO O4 detection-pipeline configuration.
    

            
    Numerical implementation
    
            
    The 64×32 spectrogram is binned to 16×8 for the sr-only fallback table. The chirp curve is overlaid analytically from the f^{−8/3} formula. Posterior medians and percentiles are taken from the GWTC-3 release where available.
    
      
    For the corner plot (extra2), the diagonal 1D histograms are Gaussian samples centered on the median with σ from the posterior catalog; the off-diagonal panels are 2D ellipses for the 90% credible region. This is a stylized representation — the actual GWTC-3 posteriors are multi-modal in some cases (GW190521, GW190814) and the dashboard does not reproduce the multi-modality.
    
      
    Source-class classification (BBH / BNS / NSBH / mass-gap) uses the chirp-mass and component-mass cuts standard in the literature: BNS if both masses < 3 M⊙; NSBH if one mass < 3 and one ≥ 3; BBH if both ≥ 3.
    

            
    Glossary & notation
    
            
    Symbols and domain terms used throughout the model. Symbol units are given where applicable.
    
            
    M
    Chirp mass = (m_1 m_2)^(3/5) / (m_1 + m_2)^(1/5). Units: M⊙
    m_1, m_2
    Component masses (m_1 ≥ m_2). Units: M⊙
    q
    Mass ratio m_2/m_1, with q ≤ 1. Units: (unitless)
    χ_eff
    Effective aligned spin (mass-weighted). Units: [-1, +1]
    D_L
    Luminosity distance. Units: Mpc or Gpc
    h(t)
    Gravitational-wave strain time-series. Units: (dimensionless)
    f(t)
    Instantaneous GW frequency. Units: Hz
    t_c
    Coalescence time. Units: s
    SNR
    Signal-to-noise ratio (matched-filter). Units: (unitless)
    FAR
    False-alarm rate; trigger rate from non-astrophysical noise. Units: 1/yr
    BBH
    Binary black hole. Units: (categorical)
    BNS
    Binary neutron star. Units: (categorical)
    NSBH
    Neutron-star–black-hole binary. Units: (categorical)
    PE
    Parameter estimation (Bayesian posterior inference). Units: (method)
    PN
    Post-Newtonian order in the inspiral expansion. Units: (integer/2)
    Chirp
    Frequency upsweep during inspiral. Units: (qualitative)
    Ringdown
    Damped quasi-normal-mode oscillation after merger. Units: (qualitative)
    

            
    Worked example
    
            
    End-to-end numerical walk-through of one Monte Carlo path for a representative event, showing how a single random draw translates through the model to a published statistic on the dashboard.
    
            
    1. Inputs. GW150914. Posterior median M = 28.6 M⊙, q = 0.80, χ_eff = −0.06, D_L = 410 Mpc, network SNR = 24. 
    2. Component masses. From M and q: m_1 + m_2 = M·(1+q)^(6/5)/q^(3/5); for M=28.6, q=0.80, total mass ≈ 65 M⊙. So m_1 ≈ 36, m_2 ≈ 29 M⊙. This matches Abbott et al. 2016 within posteriors. 
    3. Chirp track. Inspiral frequency at t = −0.20 s before merger: f(t) = (1/8π)·(5/(256·(t_c−t)))^(3/8)·(GM/c³)^(−5/8) ≈ 60 Hz. At t = −0.05 s, f ≈ 125 Hz. At merger, f_ISCO ≈ c³/(6³^(1/2) π G M_tot) ≈ 134 Hz. 
      f(t) ∝ (t_c − t)^{−3/8}
    4. Strain amplitude. Peak strain h ≈ 1.0×10⁻²¹ for GW150914 at D_L = 410 Mpc. This is what LIGO actually measured. 
    5. Source classification. Both m_1, m_2 ≥ 3 M⊙ → BBH classification. χ_eff close to zero → low/zero aligned spin. Detection-quality (SNR 24, FAR < 1/200,000 yr) → DETECTION signal. 
    

            
    Sensitivity analysis
    
            
    Tornado panel (extra3) shows that for inspiral-dominated signals the posterior is most sensitive to f_low (low-frequency cutoff): LIGO's 20 Hz cutoff vs Cosmic Explorer's 5 Hz changes the integration time from ~1 s to ~80 s for a 30 M⊙ BBH. SNR scales with sqrt(integration time). Posterior on χ_eff is highly sensitive to the merger-ringdown phase modeling. Parameter grid (extra4) sweeps (SNR, f_low) jointly.
    

            
    Comparison vs alternative approaches
    
            
    Three standard alternative modeling choices were considered before the model above was selected.
    
            
    • LALInference (nested sampling). Original LIGO PE pipeline (Veitch et al. 2015). Robust on the well-tested cases but slow for new-physics templates.
    • Bilby (stochastic sampling). Open-source replacement (Ashton et al. 2019); now the de facto LIGO standard. Faster, modular, and the source of most current GWTC-3 results.
    • Machine-learning posterior (DINGO, VITAMIN). Normalizing-flow or amortized-inference networks trained on simulated waveforms. Much faster than MCMC but harder to verify; not yet production for detection-confirmation.
    

            
    Known limitations
    
            
    • Higher post-Newtonian corrections (1PN, 2PN, 2.5PN spin-orbit) and ringdown phase are not in the simplified chirp-track display; for high-mass events the inspiral approximation is incomplete.
    • Precession and eccentricity show up only as widened posterior credible regions, not as explicit waveform features.
    • Detector noise is treated as stationary Gaussian — non-stationary glitches (real LIGO data product) are not modeled.
    • Multi-modal posteriors (e.g. GW190521, GW190814) are approximated by a single Gaussian; the dashboard does not reproduce mode separation.
    • Multi-messenger counterparts (electromagnetic, neutrino) are not displayed; the GW170817 kilonova story is not in scope here.
