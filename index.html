    Methodology & model
    
              
    Technical write-up of the stochastic process, calibration approach, and published references for the Event layer of this dashboard.
    
            
    
            WCAG 2.2 AA · dissertation-grade documentation
          
    

          
    
            
    Stochastic model
    
            
    For a compact-binary inspiral, the leading-order post-Newtonian gravitational-wave frequency evolution depends only on the chirp mass M = (m_1 m_2)^{3/5} / (m_1 + m_2)^{1/5}:
    
      
    f^{−8/3}(t) = (8/3) (5/256)^{8/3} (π G M / c³)^{5/3} (t_c − t)
    
      
    The strain amplitude scales as h ∝ M^{5/3} f^{2/3} / D_L. From the observed frequency-time chirp track and amplitude, joint Bayesian inference yields posteriors on {M, q, χ_eff, D_L}.
    

            
    Calibration
    
            
    Per-event median posteriors are seeded from the LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA GWTC-3 catalog (Abbott et al. 2023). The Q-transform spectrogram template uses a synthetic Gaussian envelope in (log f, t) centered on the analytic chirp track with width σ proportional to the posterior parameter uncertainty. The corner plot diagonals are Gaussian marginals centered on the median; off-diagonals show 90% credible-region ellipses.
    

            
    Numerical implementation
    
            
    The 64×32 spectrogram is binned to 16×8 for the sr-only fallback table. The chirp curve is overlaid analytically from the f^{−8/3} formula above. Posterior medians and percentiles are taken from the GWTC-3 release where available; O4 candidates use placeholder values until the catalog finalizes.
    

            
    Known limitations
    
            
    • Higher post-Newtonian corrections (1PN, 2PN) and ringdown phase are not in the chirp-track display; for high-mass events the inspiral approximation is incomplete.
    • Precession and eccentricity show up only as widened posterior credible regions, not as explicit waveform features.
    • Detector noise is treated as stationary Gaussian — non-stationary glitches (real LIGO data product) are not modeled.
    

            
    References
    
            
    1. Abbott, B. P. et al. (LIGO Scientific Collaboration and Virgo Collaboration). (2016). Observation of Gravitational Waves from a Binary Black Hole Merger. Physical Review Letters 116, 061102.
    2. Abbott, R. et al. (LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA). (2023). GWTC-3: Compact Binary Coalescences Observed by LIGO and Virgo. Physical Review X 13.
    3. Cutler, C. & Flanagan, É. E. (1994). Gravitational waves from merging compact binaries. Physical Review D 49, 2658.
    4. Veitch, J. et al. (2015). Parameter estimation for compact binaries with ground-based gravitational-wave observations. Physical Review D 91, 042003.
    5. Chatziioannou, K. (2020). Neutron-star tidal deformabilities and equation of state. General Relativity and Gravitation 52.
